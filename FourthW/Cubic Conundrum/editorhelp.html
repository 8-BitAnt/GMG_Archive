Editor Help

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

General:

Press 'Space' to toggle the cursor on and off.

Press 'C' to change camera modes (just like in the main game).

Press 'Ctrl' to change movement modes (Controls follow the camera or not).

Press 'Tab' to reset the camera to its original position.


When you have an object selected you will see it in you marker.

Click the left mouse button or press 'Enter' to place the selected object.

Click the right mouse button or press 'Delete' to remove any object in the position of your marker.


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Explanation of Editor buttons from left to right:


1. File: Contains options dealing with the editor and level itself. Can also be accessed by pressing 'F1'.

     - New: Clears the current level to start creating a new one.

     - Open: Allows you to select a '.lvl' file to open in the editor.

     - Save: Exports the current level as a '.lvl' file to be used in the main game.

     -Exit: Closes the editor and returns you to the main screen.


2. Edit: Contains objects to select and add to your levels. Can also be accessed by pressing 'F2'.

     - None: Deselects all objects.

     - Givers: Opens a sub-menu that shows 'Giving objects'. More in section 2.A.

     - Takers: Opens a sub-menu that shows 'Taking objects'. More in section 2.B.

     - Others: Opens a sub-menu that shows misc. objects and obstacles. More in section 2.C.


3. Help: Contains the help file. Can also be accessed by pressing 'F3'.

     - Help: Opens the text you're viewing now.


4. Level Buttons: Change the height of the objects you are placing in your level.

     - There are only five height levels. You can change the height level by clicking one of the buttons,   

               using the scroll wheel on the mouse, or pressing 'Q' to lower the level and 'E' to raise the level.


5. Type Buttons: Change the type of the object you are placing in your level.

      - There are up to three types for every object (Normal, Alt, and second Alt). The different types of

               each object are explained in sections 2.A, 2.B, and 2.C. You can change the type by clicking one

               of the buttons or by pressing '1' for 'Normal' type, '2' for 'Alt' type, and '3' for second 'Alt'

               type.


6. Grid and Marker Buttons: Toggle the grid and marker displays.

     - Clicking the grid button or pressing 'Shift' will turn the grid on or off.

     - Clicking the marker button will turn the position markers for your cursor on or off.


7. Object Shift buttons: Move all objects placed in the level one space in the direction clicked.

     - The buttons do not move with the camera (I.E. clicking the up arrow will always shift objects towards

               the top of the level).


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------


2.A. Givers: Objects that give their color to your cube.

     - Selecting one allows you to place a 'Giver' in your level. The color you select will be a 'Giver' of

               that color ('Normal' is the standard gray block). Their 'Normal' type has color on all sides

               and the top, 'Alt' type has color only on the sides, second 'Alt' only has color on the top.


2.B. Takers: Objects that need to be filled with the correct color to activate their paired obstacles. Also   

               called 'Need' objects.

     - Selecting one allows you to place a 'Taker' in your level. The color you select will be a 'Taker' of

               that color. Their 'Normal' type has a 'Need' on all sides and the top, 'Alt' type has a 'Need'

               only on the sides, second 'Alt' has a 'Need' only on the top.


2.C. Others: All other objects and obstacles activated by the 'Takers'.

     - Selecting a Lift/Pillar object allows you to place an elevator or pillar of the color you choose in

               your level. Their 'Normal' type is the elevator, 'Alt' type is the pillar starting in the up     

               position, second 'Alt' type is the pillar starting in the down position.

     - Blank Block: Lets you place Blank blocks in your level. Their 'Normal' type is 'Blank' on all

               sides and the top, 'Alt' type is 'Blank' only on the sides, second 'Alt' type is 'Blank' only on

               the top.

     - Rotate Tile: Lets you place rotate tiles in your level. They can only be placed on the floor.

               Their 'Normal' type rotates the cube clock-wise while the 'Alt' type rotates the cube counter     

               clock-wise. There is no second 'Alt' type.

     - Clear Tile: Lets you place clear tiles in your level. They can only be placed on the floor.

               There are no 'Alt' types for clear tiles.

     - Start Block: This is where your cube enters the level. There must be one (and only one) in every

               level. Placing more that one in a level can cause errors.

     - End Block: This is where you cube leaves the level and is how you 'win' a level. There must be

               one (and only one) in every level. Placing more than one in a level can cause errors.
